# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want Chikaima to be a welcoming, respectful project for contributors, users, maintainers, and anyone participating in issues, discussions, pull requests, docs, or community spaces.

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, level of experience, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity or orientation, or technology preferences.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that helps this community:

- using welcoming and inclusive language
- being respectful of different viewpoints and experience levels
- giving and receiving constructive feedback
- focusing criticism on ideas, code, docs, and outcomes rather than people
- assuming good intent while still being clear about impact
- acknowledging mistakes and making reasonable corrections
- keeping discussions relevant to Chikaima and its users

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, or sustained disruption
- insulting, demeaning, or personal attacks
- sexualized language or imagery in project spaces
- unwanted sexual attention or advances
- publishing someone else's private information without explicit permission
- trolling or deliberately derailing project discussions
- pressuring people to disclose private credentials, provider keys, private media, or sensitive deployment details
- any conduct that maintainers reasonably judge inappropriate in a professional open source project

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all Chikaima project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and reviews
- project documentation and examples
- community chats or forums associated with the project
- public events or online spaces where someone is representing Chikaima

It also applies when behavior outside project spaces affects someone's ability to participate safely in the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the project maintainers through a private channel when one is available. If the project has no dedicated private reporting address yet, open a concise GitHub issue asking for a maintainer contact method without including sensitive details.

Please include as much relevant context as you feel safe sharing, such as links, screenshots, dates, participants, and a short description of what happened.

Maintainers will handle reports with care and will avoid sharing identifying details except as needed to review, respond, or meet legal obligations.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards and may take appropriate action in response to behavior they judge harmful or inappropriate.

Possible actions include:

- a private clarification or warning
- requesting edits to comments, issues, pull requests, or docs
- temporarily limiting participation
- closing, locking, or deleting project discussions
- banning a participant from project spaces
- reporting serious threats or illegal behavior to the appropriate platform or authority

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers should apply this code of conduct consistently and fairly. They should also model the standards they expect from contributors, especially during disagreement or review.

## Attribution

This code of conduct is adapted from common open source community standards, including the Contributor Covenant, and tailored for the Chikaima project.
